# Inductor Response (RL Circuit Simulation)

This tutorial demonstrates how an **inductor responds to an AC signal** in a simple RL series circuit.  
By applying a 40 Hz AC source to a 1 H inductor and 180 Ω resistor, we observe how the inductor creates a phase‑shifted voltage waveform.
The current through the inductor is always zero.
---

## 🔧 Circuit Components
- **AC Source:** 40 Hz
- **Inductor:** 1 H
- **Resistor:** 180 Ω
- **Configuration:** Series RL circuit

---

## ⚙️ How It Works
1. The AC source applies a sinusoidal voltage to the RL series circuit.  
2. The inductor resists changes in current, causing a **phase shift** between voltage and current.  
3. At low frequencies (like 40 Hz), the inductor’s reactance is moderate, so the waveform is still clearly sinusoidal.  
4. The simulation graph shows the resulting voltage across the inductor.

This is a fundamental example of **AC reactance** and **inductive behavior**.

---

## 📊 Simulation Output
- **Max Voltage:** ~4.1 V  
- **Min Voltage:** ~–4.1 V  
- **RMS Voltage:** ~2.9 V  
- **Frequency:** 40 Hz  
- **Time Scale:** 2 ms/div  
- **Waveform:** Clean sinusoid with phase shift
